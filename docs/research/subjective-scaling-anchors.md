# Subjective Scaling & Anchors for Energy/Mood Tracking

## Problem
A 1–10 scale is often too abstract, leading to "decision paralysis" or inconsistent data. Users struggle to distinguish between a "6" and a "7," which makes tracking feel like a chore.

## Solution: State Anchoring
Instead of abstract numbers, the scale should be anchored to specific, recognizable physiological and psychological states.

### Anchor Map
*   **1–2 (Critical):** Hard to function. Overwhelming anxiety, anger, or exhaustion. Noticeable to others.
*   **3–4 (Sub-par):** Dragging through the day. Doing what's required but without engagement. "Wishing the day would end."
*   **5–6 (Neutral):** Standard day. Neither good nor bad. Functional and steady.
*   **7–8 (Positive):** Good energy and focus. Feeling of satisfaction. Wanting to do more.
*   **9–10 (Peak):** Rare "Flow" state. Complete presence and high effectiveness.

### Practical Evaluation Prompts
*   **For Energy:** "If I had to do something right now, do I have the strength for it?"
*   **For Mood:** "How do I feel about what's happening around me?"

## Implementation Principle: The 5-Second Rule
The evaluation should be honest and fast. The first feeling is usually the most accurate for trend analysis. Accuracy in a single day is less important than the 90-day trend.
